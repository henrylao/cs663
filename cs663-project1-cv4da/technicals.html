The Traffic Scene Understanding Problem

Foreword

People are extremely adept at visually perceiving scenes and understanding scene structures. Recently, scene understanding has become on of the challenging and perhaps most important problem in computer vision.

Images contain visual information; however, such information can contain various features such as shapes, edges, textures and colors.

In scene understanding, the primary motivation driving object detection is to establish what objects are present and where they are located for extraction of information. Understanding of a scene requires integration of information at various levels for the extraction of semantic relationships and patterns from observations.

What is scene understanding to Assistive Driver-Assistance Systems (ADAS)?

Scene understanding is the core technology -- it is the brain behind ADAS and is extremely important for smart or autonomous vehicles.

Issue 1: Generalization

Most models have been customized for recognizing specific categories of traffic instead of general traffic targets. We have systems that are pretty good are good at one particular task like traffic sign detection, vehicle detection, or people detection; however, we don't have a system that is really good at generalizing given new complex variations of a scene.

Issue 2: Lack of Driver Assistance

Most recognition modules have been trained to perform the task of identifying objects. This does not address the primary issue -- provision of driver suggestions and scene description. Image captioning can serve as a solution to this problem providing useful descriptions of traffic situations and assist with ADAS when handling for edge cases in traffic.

Issue 3: Poor Integration of Recognition Modules

Naturally, one would think that more modules would provide an advantage -- an improvement to decisions for driving functions. This is not the case with numerous independent modules resulting with the issue with fusing Mutlimodal information to generate a decision for driving operations within a complicated traffic scene. The inability to fuse the various categories of information into a cohesive representation of a scene or overarching concept is an obstacle to overcome.

Mutlimodal Information

Mutlimodal information refers to data that come from different modalities. In the case of recognition modules, we consider each module to be different forms of information to be related and integrated. People experience the world visually, audibly, through touch, smell and taste. All of which define a perceptual modality in which signals are obtained to be processed.
