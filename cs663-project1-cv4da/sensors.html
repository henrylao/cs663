Sensor Possibilities

There are various sensors to be used to define the "eyes" for the computer vision system. Specifically, there are 2 types of sensors (active and passive) for detection of obstacles within the scene and funneling of information from the surroundings

  • More Info [5]

Radar

Radars are detection systems that utilize radio waves to determine the distance (range), angle, or velocity of obstacles. Radio raves are transmitted to be reflected off an obstructing object and returned to the receiver for information gathering about the object's location as well as speed. They are useful for providing a signal for long distances in poor visibility conditions.

Lidar

Lidars are detection systems that utilize lasers to gather information about the surrounding area by measuring the time for the reflected light to return to the receiver. They are most useful for long distance vision during ideal conditions (no rain or fog)

Ultrasonic Sensor

Ultrasonic sensors are detection systems that employ sound to detect movement of targets and measure distance of objects in an environment. They are most useful when detecting the presence of obstacles in a very short range especially transparent and non-metallic obstructions.

Camera (Android)

In general, cameras provide a wide view -- a perspective of color when ingesting an environment. Furthermore, they are easy to deploy and are cheap in contrast to Lidars

Far-Infrared (FIR) Camera

FIR cameras utilize thermographic cameras to sense infrared radiation. This allows for perception in low light environments. Furthermore, lighting conditions and obstacle surface properties do not affect FIR cameras.
