Progress in deep learning has led to the employment of more modern algorithms such as Regional Convolutional Neural Networks (R-CNN) [4], [11], [15] and Faster R-CNN [10]

Faster-RCNN

A comparison of the two architectures along with others demonstrates the speed of Faster R-CNN to its predecessors suggesting it can be used for real-time object detection. Considering pedestrian detection, speed of a model along with accuracy is of utmost importance when accounting for safety.

In the pursuit of speed, the You-Only-Look-Once (YOLO) architecture [9] is worth mentioning as it is orders of magnitude faster than the prior object detection algorithms. Unfortunately, it struggles with small objects within frame due to spatial constraints of the algorithm.
