Future Work

Handling for Cases of Occlusion

Although not explicitly specified, it is heavily implied that the proposed approach utilizes a regular camera for signal ingestion; however, typical camera's do not have the benefits of FIR Camera's to deal with reduced light environments. More work needs to be carried out to handle for multimodal data.

Consideration of Variations of CNN-RNN

Smaller networks such as MobileNetV1 as well as EfficientNet can be considered for the CNN. For the RNN portion, Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU) layers can be explored as well as LSTMs with Attention; however, care should be taken when using LSTMs with Attention since they utilize a memory-hungry architecture known as a transformer.

Toward's a Gold-Standard for NLP Benchmarking

It is evident that there are disagreements with the existing NLP/MT benchmarking methods; thus, it is clear that there exists more work to be done when attempting to define a subsitute -- a proxy for human evaluation. For now, BLEU remains the supposed gold-standard when working in the NLP/MT space; however, there exist other forms of benchmarks. Future work should include such benchmarks to assess their validity in serving as a proxy of what can be universally accepeted as human evaluation.

Integration of Detected Lane Lines

The lines of the lane provide information particular to a given moment within the scene that may be valuable to making sense of a series of scenes. Of course, this is likely to be challenging when dealing with inner-city high-traffic situations where in which there are multiple objects within a scene ocluding the lane lines. Additional sensors can be considered, but would further exarcerbate the issue for in need of a sensor fusion solution. In addition, with more data comes the need for more processing thus creating a necessity for more powerful compact hardware. More work needs to be done here in evaluating the value of adding an additional feature such as lane lines to this system in terms of image captioning as well as what market is fit for such a tool.

Repurposing as a Driver's Perspectice Analysis Tool

Although initially proposed as a driving assistant, it is clear that the utility provided by such an assistant in reducing car accidents is limited. Further development and integration with vehicle would be necessary to integrate with control systems such as auto braking. Distracted driving is a major leading cause of accidents and with such a tool, it makes me wonder to what extent would such a proposed assistant actually provide utility and to what extent would it distract drivers in transit.
